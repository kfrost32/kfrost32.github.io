Web 2.0

I conducted over 40 hours of phone interviews + additional qualitative surveys and analytics evaluations to help identify pressing issues and trends amongst our community members. The transcriptions and notes of these interviews were concatenated into a single doc which broke down the most prominent feedback into overarching themes. After discussion with the full team, each theme was then given a series of actionable design, dev, and business decisions to help us address the issues at hand.

One of the key changes we made was updating the homepage with an increased focus on search and discovery. We found that while most of our community was happy and familiar with the features of the site, a portion of first time users had a very narrow view into what The Outbound was capable of. As we continued to grow internationally, it was important for us to position The Outbound as an adventure travel tool, rather than a publication or "hiking in your backyard" focus.

I also discovered that our search tool was woefully incomplete; community members were interested in searching all our content around destinations, not just adventures. We built new search filters, and improved our surfacing of related content across the site.
